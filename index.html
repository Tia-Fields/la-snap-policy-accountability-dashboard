Payment Error Definitions

Overpayment

Benefits issued in excess of household eligibility.

Underpayment

Eligible benefits that were not correctly issued to the household.

Nature of Errors

Errors are technical inaccuracies (62% unintentional household, 14% administrative) and are distinct from intentional fraud.

A SNAP payment error rate measures how often benefits are calculated incorrectly, whether households receive too much or too little assistance due to administrative mistakes in eligibility or benefit calculations.

Federal Threshold

6.0%

The USDA standard for accuracy. Exceeding 6% triggers mandatory corrective actions and fiscal penalties.

Analyst's Perspective

Effective administrative accuracy requires targeted investments in IT modernization and analyst training. High error rates are symptomatic of technical complexity rather than intentional misuse.

The transition of SNAP functions between agencies during the 2026 Session presents a critical juncture for ensuring program stability and protecting state resources.

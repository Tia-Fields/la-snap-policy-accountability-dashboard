Payment Error Definitions

Overpayment

Benefits issued in excess of household eligibility.

Underpayment

Eligible benefits that were not correctly issued to the household.

Nature of Errors

Administrative Errors are technical inaccuracies resulting from complex procedural requirements. When households lose help due to red tape (Procedural Closures), federal audits often classify these gaps as Admin Errors, directly inflating the state's error rate regardless of the household's actual eligibility.

A SNAP payment error rate measures how often benefits are calculated incorrectly, whether households receive too much or too little assistance due to administrative mistakes in eligibility or benefit calculations.

Fiscal Impact of Administrative Friction

State data show that when Procedural Closures reach 82.2 percent, many households lose benefits because of paperwork barriers and administrative processes rather than a true loss of eligibility. When federal Quality Control auditors review these cases, they are often classified as Administrative Errors. This administrative failure, not fraud, contributes to Louisiana’s 7.61 percent SNAP error rate, exceeding the federal 6 percent threshold and exposing the state to an estimated 95.1 million dollar liability under the Fiscal Responsibility Act (H.R. 1).
